Virat Kohli Career Beginnings

In 2002, Kohli embarked on his early cricketing journey when he joined the Delhi Under-15 squad to compete in the Polly Umrigar Trophy.

In the subsequent season, he assumed the role of team captain.

Kohli continued to showcase his talent, representing the Delhi Under-17 team in the Vijay Merchant Trophy during the 2004-05 season, where they emerged as champions.

In early 2006, Kohli marked his List A debut for Delhi, and later that same year, he made his maiden appearance in first-class cricket for the same team.

By the end of the season, he had amassed a total of 257 runs.

During the summer of 2006, Kohli became a part of the India Under-19 squad for their tour of England, where the team triumphed in both series against the England Under-19s.

The following year, he made his debut in Twenty20 cricket and also toured Sri Lanka with the India Under-19s.

In early 2008, Kohli assumed the captaincy of the victorious Indian team in the Under-19 Cricket World Cup.

Virat Kohli Career (Part 1)

In 2008, Kohli made his debut in international cricket for the Indian ODI squad during their tour of Sri Lanka, where India won the series with a 3-2 scoreline.

After recovering from a shoulder injury, he returned to the national team in the 2009 ICC Champions Trophy.

Subsequently, he participated in the seven-match home ODI series against Australia and the home ODI series against Sri Lanka.

Early in 2010, Kohli excelled in the tri-nation ODI tournament in Bangladesh, emerging as the top run-scorer with 275 runs.

He continued his impressive form in the tri-series in Zimbabwe, becoming the fastest Indian batsman to reach 1,000 runs in ODI cricket.

Later, he played a pivotal role in India’s 5-0 series win against New Zealand, finishing as the leading run-scorer in ODIs for that year, with 995 runs.

Kohli’s remarkable performances earned him a spot in India’s 2011 World Cup squad. During the tournament, he scored an unbeaten century and secured his fifth ODI century.

India went on to win the World Cup, their first victory since 1983.

Later in the same year, Kohli dominated the five-match home ODI series against England, contributing to India’s 5-0 series win.

In the subsequent ODI series against the West Indies, he recorded his eighth ODI century, ending the year as the top run-scorer in ODI cricket with 1,381 runs.

Following a strong showing in Australia, Kohli was appointed as the vice-captain of the Indian team for the 2012 Asia Cup in Bangladesh.

Despite India not reaching the final, Kohli finished the tournament as the highest run-scorer, accumulating 357 runs.

He continued his success in the five-match ODI tour of Sri Lanka during the summer and also in the ICC World Twenty20.

Virat Kohli Career (Part 2)

In early 2013, India achieved a clean sweep against Australia in a home Test series.

A few months afterward, India emerged victorious against England in the ICC Champions Trophy.

This success led to Kohli taking the helm as captain of the Indian team for the triangular series in the West Indies.

During the subsequent five-match ODI tour in Zimbabwe, India continued their winning streak by sweeping Zimbabwe 5-0.

The team’s triumphant run extended to a seven-match ODI series against Australia.

In December 2014, Kohli assumed the role of Test captain for the first time during the Australian tour.

During the summer of 2015, he secured his inaugural Test series win as captain on the tour of Bangladesh.

The following year, Kohli played a pivotal role in India’s victory in the Asia Cup.

In 2017, Kohli achieved a remarkable feat by amassing 2,818 international runs, ranking as the third-highest ever in a single year and setting a new record for an Indian player.

In 2018, during the tour of South Africa, India dominated the ODI series with a 5-1 win.

The team continued their winning streak with victories in the tour of England and the Test series in Australia.

In 2019, Kohli took charge as captain of the Indian squad for the Cricket World Cup.

He later led the national team in the 2021 ICC World Test Championship Final and the 2021 ICC Men’s T20 World Cup.

Following the conclusion of the latter tournament, Kohli announced his decision to step down from his role as ODI captain.

Subsequently, in early 2022, he also relinquished his position as Test captain.

Virat Kohli Investments

At the age of 25, Virat Kohli embarked on his journey as an investor by acquiring stakes in a London-based social media startup known as Sports Convo.

The startup’s primary goal was to bridge the gap between sports stars and their fan base.

This initiative came two years after Kohli had launched the ‘Virat Kohli Foundation,’ an organization dedicated to providing sports facilities and support to aspiring athletes from across the nation.

In 2019, Virat made a significant investment in Galactus Funware Technology Pvt. Ltd, a Bengaluru-based company that owns the fantasy sports website Mobile Premier League (MPL).

Kohli’s investment portfolio extends to the fashion industry, where he has put substantial capital into a startup called Universal SportsBiz (USPL).

His investment in USPL amounted to a substantial INR 19.30 Crores in October 2020.

Furthermore, Kohli’s ventures include ownership of gyms, fitness centers, and restaurants spread across the country.

One of his most noteworthy recent investments is in Digit Insurance, an online insurance aggregator currently valued at an impressive $3.5 billion.

Notably, the cricketer has also invested in Puma’s One8 brand, Chisel Fitness, and Wrogn, a fitness brand.

Additionally, he holds a co-ownership position in the ISL team FC Goa, alongside Jaydev Mody and Akshay Tandon.
