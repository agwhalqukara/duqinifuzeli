Know About Olivia Jade; Parents, Age, Sister, Boyfriend, College

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, September 11, 2024

Sometimes all you need to get recognition is to bank the fame of your successful parents. Same is the case for the daughter of actress Lori Loughlin, Olivia Jade, who is recently caught up in the college admission scandal.

Besides being the daughter of an actress mother and fashion designer father, Olivia Jade holds her own identity as an improving YouTuber. She turned up the social media heat since launching her YouTube Channel back in August 2014.

What is Olivia Jade doing now? Who is her boyfriend? Explore more about her age, college, sister, height, and interesting facts. 

Olivia Jade Bio & Family: Age, Parents, Sister

Olivia Jade, age 21, was born Olivia jade Giannulli on 28 September 1999 to her parents, Lori Loughlin and Mossimo Giannulli. While her mother, Lori marks fame for appearing as Rebecca Donaldson-Katsopolis on the ABC sitcom Full House (1988-1995), her father stands out as a successful fashion designer.

Olivia Jade pictured with her mother, Lori Loughlin in 2018 (Picture: New York Times)

Olivia shares a sibling bond with her elder sister, Isabella Rose Giannulli, who was born on 16 September 1998. Similarly, she has an older brother named Gianni Giannulli.  

The talented social media celebrity stands tall at a height of 5 feet and 4 inches (1.62 meters) and weighs around 103 pounds (47 kg). 

Olivia Jade Social Media Career; YouTube Star

Olivia stormed into a social media career by launching her YouTube channel in August 2014. She shares makeup tutorials, and vlogs about her college life on her channel which is nearing its 2 Million subscribers. In these years, the social media star has collaborated with popular fellow YouTubers David Dobrik, Trisha Paytas, and Emma Chamberlain.

Meantime, Olivia owns a makeup brand named Olivia x Sephora Collection Bronze & Illuminate Palette which she launched in December 2018.  Apart from that, she previously worked with the online shopping website Princess Polly in November 2018.

Net Worth & Earnings

Though the actual net worth of Olivia Jade remains far from known, her yearly YouTube earning through a self-titled channel falls between $2.8K  and $44K. Besides, she equally enjoys the earnings of her mother and father who possess $8 Million and $80 Million respectively.

Introduces Singer Boyfriend Through YouTube Video!

The charming lady on YouTube introduced her subscribers to her singer boyfriend, Jackson Guthy on 17 February 2019. Jackson is a 25-year-old American singer/songwriter best known for his 2012 single Make Up Your Mind. However, it is now clear now if the couple is still dating each other. 

Surfaced Around College Admission Scandal

Olivia Jade’s parents were among the dozens to have been charged by the FBI and U.S. Attorney’s Office amidst a large-scale nationwide college entrance exam cheating scandal on 12 March 2019.

The allegation stated that her parents agreed to pay bribes worth $500K in exchange for having Olivia and her sister designated as recruits to the University of Southern California. The Desperate Housewives star, Felicity Huffman was among the ones to be revolved around a similar scandal.

Consequences of the Scandal

The major consequence of Olivia’s involvement in the college admissions scandal was the cancellation of many of her lucrative sponsorship deals. As a YouTuber, she had landed partnerships with major companies including Sephora and TRESemmé; both severed ties with her within a few days of the scandal’s surfacing.

Similarly, Olivia immediately stopped posting content on her YouTube and social media pages following the scandal. However, she returned with two fresh videos in December 2019. Later on, Olivia resumed her social media career and continued posting regularly on her Instagram account as well. 

Some Interesting Facts About Olivia Jade

  • Olivia is also popular on Instagram and Twitter over 1.3 Million and 157k followers respectively.
  • She faced criticism for saying she went to college for games and parties in August 2018. She later apologized through her another YouTube video.
